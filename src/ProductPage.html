Granite Description

Property Description
Type Granite is a type of igneous rock that is predominantly composed of quartz and feldspar with minor amounts of mica, amphiboles, and other minerals.
Color Granite comes in a variety of colors depending on its mineral composition, ranging from white and gray to pink, red, green, and black.
Texture It typically has a coarse-grained texture due to its slow crystallization from magma beneath the Earth's surface.
Origin Formed from the slow crystallization of magma deep underground, granite is found in many parts of the world and is often used in construction and decoration.
Usage Granite is commonly used as a building material for countertops, flooring, monuments, and other decorative purposes due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.
